Our Mission

At Dream Time, we aim to take unfortunate Indigenous people that have ended up on the streets and give them a chance at restarting their lives.
Our goal is to identify two homeless Aboriginal people every month, and help them find employment to earn a sustainable income. We will provide temporary shelter, food and clothing while they look for work.

The Problem

According to the Australian Bureau of Statistics, there are currently over 10 000 homeless Indigenous Australians. That makes up about 12% of the homeless people in Australia, however, the Aboriginal population only makes up 3.3% of Australia. Indigenous people are four times more likely to be homeless than their non-Indigenous counterpart, and we aim to change that.

Our Steps to Success

We first identify two homeless Indigenous Australians every month and take them in. We provide them with food, and a two-bedroom apartment two live in for one month. While they are staying there, we give them each a new suit and a new set of casual clothes. Finally, we hire a proffesional to come in and help in writing a resume for them. By taking care of basic needs, we can give these people a chance to focus on earning a sustainable income, rather than finding food and shelter. Additionally, we offer support to any social or psycological problems they may contend with. All of these steps combined can help change their life for the better.
